What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure that uses your height and weight to work out if your weight is healthy.

BMI Range Weight Status
Below 18.5 Underweight
18.5 – 24.9 Healthy Weight
25.0 – 29.9 Overweight
30.0 and Above Obesity
The BMI Calculator helps assess your Body Mass Index (BMI), which is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. As shown in the chart below, BMI values are calculated by combining your height and weight measurements to determine which health category you fall into. It's a simple yet effective tool widely used to identify weight categories that may lead to health problems.

Why is BMI Important?

Health Assessment

BMI is a simple tool to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems, providing a general indicator of potential risks.

Population Studies

Researchers use BMI to track obesity trends, helping to form public health policies and initiatives for entire populations.

Personal Goal Setting

Knowing your BMI is a starting point for setting personal health goals, in consultation with a healthcare provider.

Limitations of BMI

Muscle vs. Fat

BMI does not differentiate between body fat and muscle mass. Muscular individuals can be classified as overweight.

Fat Distribution

It doesn't account for where fat is stored. Visceral fat (around the abdomen) is more harmful than fat elsewhere.

Population-Specific

Standard BMI ranges are based on Western populations and may not be accurate for people of different ethnic backgrounds.

Healthy Tips & Precautions

Health Tips & Precautions

Healthy Eating

  • Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins
  • Control portion sizes to avoid overeating
  • Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive salt
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Practice mindful eating - pay attention to hunger and fullness cues

Physical Activity

  • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
  • Include both cardio and strength training exercises
  • Find activities you enjoy to make exercise sustainable
  • Break up sitting time with short movement breaks
  • Gradually increase intensity and duration to avoid injury

Lifestyle Factors

  • Get 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption
  • Schedule regular health check-ups
  • Set realistic health goals and track progress
