What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure that uses your height and weight to work out if your weight is healthy.

BMI Range Weight Status
Below 18.5 Underweight
18.5 – 24.9 Healthy Weight
25.0 – 29.9 Overweight
30.0 and Above Obesity
The BMI Calculator helps assess your Body Mass Index (BMI), which is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. As shown in the chart below, BMI values are calculated by combining your height and weight measurements to determine which health category you fall into. It's a simple yet effective tool widely used to identify weight categories that may lead to health problems.

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful tool, it has limitations. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, overall body composition, and racial and sex differences. For example, athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI but not an unhealthy amount of body fat.
It’s important to use BMI as one part of a broader health assessment, alongside other factors like waist-to-hip ratio, diet, physical activity, and overall wellness.

Why Monitor Your BMI?

Monitoring your BMI can help you maintain a healthy weight, which is crucial for reducing the risk of chronic conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension. It also encourages better awareness of your physical health and promotes proactive lifestyle adjustments.
Regularly checking your BMI, especially during life stages like menopause or aging, can help you better understand how your body changes over time and adapt accordingly.
